"Twilight" by Stephenie Meyer is a supernatural romance novel about a teenage girl named Bella Swan who moves to the small town of Forks, Washington, to live with her father. There, she becomes enthralled by Edward Cullen, a mysterious and handsome student who turns out to be a vampire. Despite the dangers of their love and the complications of living in a world where vampires and werewolves exist, Bella and Edward's relationship deepens. The novel explores themes of love, danger, and the supernatural, setting the stage for the subsequent books in the series.

"Percy Jackson & The Olympians: The Lightning Thief" by Rick Riordan follows Percy Jackson, a twelve-year-old boy who discovers he is a demigod, the son of Poseidon. After learning of his divine heritage, Percy is thrust into a world of Greek mythology and discovers that Zeus's lightning bolt has been stolen. To prevent a catastrophic war among the gods, Percy embarks on a quest with his friends Annabeth Chase and Grover Underwood to find the stolen bolt and uncover the real thief. The adventure takes them across the United States and introduces them to various mythological creatures and challenges.

Alongside his friends Piper McLean, daughter of Aphrodite, and Leo Valdez, son of Hephaestus, Jason embarks on a quest to rescue the goddess Hera, who has been captured by the giants. 201612+ 368 pagesFiction

"Two Summers" by Aimee Friedman is a contemporary young adult novel that explores the concept of parallel lives and the choices that shape them. The story follows a teenage girl named Summer who finds herself living two distinct versions of her summer vacation. In one reality, she is spending the summer in the Hamptons with her family, while in another, she is in Paris with her best friend. As she navigates these dual experiences, Summer encounters different relationships, challenges, and opportunities. The novel delves into themes of self-discovery, the impact of choices, and the idea of finding one's true path.
